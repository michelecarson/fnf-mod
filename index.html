Hospital Havoc
Artist's Statement

Hospital Havoc takes an existing mod, “Friday Night Funkin’: Mid Fight Masses”, and turns the story of a “nun” recruiting visitors to her church, to be one of a nurse attempting to coerce her visitors to receive a routine check-up they did not sign up for. I selected Friday Night Funkin’ because I knew I could realistically mod this game in the given timeframe and create enough of a visual modification to alter the mood of the preexisting story. I altered the dark color palette of the “nun”, Sarvente, to be brighter, and more cheerful, and emphasize the cross pattern on her clothing to look more like what we envision hospital crosses to look like. Additionally, I chose Friday Night Funkin’ to mod because of my affinity for rhythm games and because of how fun it is to watch as the audience.

To create this mod, I downloaded the zipped mod file from Newgrounds and searched for the images folder where I knew I could find the sprite sheets. From there, I opened the sprite sheet in Adobe Photoshop and used the preexisting color palette to create a brighter version of it. That way, Sarvente would not look out of place with her original background. I utilized the eyedropper and paint bucket tool to speed up this process, but some sections overlapped with each other, resulting in the entire portion being the same color. To correct this, I utilized the brush tool and recolored each sprite image by hand with my iPad and Apple Pencil. After I completed this, I exported the new sprite sheet in the same file format as the original and named it to be the same name as the original sprite sheet. This allowed the mod to pick up this new sprite sheet file in the same way it did the original and modified the appearance of Sarvente in the game.

In terms of modifying Friday Night Funkin’, I did not modify the original Friday Night Funkin’ game itself, but I instead modded a preexisting mod of Friday Night Funkin’. I was led to mod this mod because of how much I enjoyed the creativity of “Mid Fight Masses” and the uniqueness of its art style and music. I thoroughly enjoyed modding “Mid Fight Masses” and I believe it is a fun representation of the kind of work I enjoy.
